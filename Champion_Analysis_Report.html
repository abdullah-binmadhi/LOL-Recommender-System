Executive Summary

The analysis of the League of Legends champion roster reveals a diverse but structured ecosystem. Fighters dominate the class distribution, accounting for 28.1% of all champions, followed by Mages (21.6%) and Marksmen (16.4%), while Assassins and Supports are the most exclusive classes at 9.9% each. In terms of combat range, the roster is remarkably balanced, with a slight majority of Melee (52.6%) champions compared to Ranged (47.4%). Accessibility appears to be a key design philosophy, as 53.8% of champions are classified as Medium difficulty, and nearly 30% are Low difficulty, leaving only a small fraction (16.4%) as highly complex. Furthermore, the release history indicates a shift from rapid expansion in the game's early years (peaking at 40 champions in 2009) to a more curated release schedule of 3-6 champions annually in the last decade, suggesting a focus on quality and balance over quantity.
